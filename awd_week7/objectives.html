Objectives:
All students will successful do the following:

  • Use technology to enhance learning
  • Use the Internet as a learning tool
  • Demonstrate scientific and logical thinking skills to solve real-life problems
  • Improve upon higher order thinking skills
  • Demonstrate proficiency in computational procedures
  • Apply problem solving strategies to find solutions
  • Understand the relevance of Physics in real-life problems
  • Work in a cooperative environment to collaborate and learn from each other
  • Choose a resource that will accommodate individual learning styles

All teachers will successfully do the following:

  • Supplement traditional “wet labs” with the use of technology
  • Assign labs for homework or long-term projects
  • Provide the students with a alternate methods of assessment
  • Spend less on maintenance and replacement of science lab equipment
  • Not have worries about safety hazards

        

CONTENT OBJECTIVES:

  • Newtonian Mechanics
    • Velocity, distance, acceleration, time in 1-D and 2-D
    • Circular and Satellite motion
    • Impulse and change in momentum
    • Kinetic and Potential Energy and the Conservation of Energy
    • Forces
  • Electricity and Magnetism
    • Ohm’s Law (V=IR), Power & Watt’s Law (P=IV)
    • Electrical Circuits in Series and Parallel (finding resistance, current, and voltage)
    • Magnetic and Electric fields
  • Waves and Optics
    •  Simple Harmonic Motion
    • Waves – wave fronts and behavior of waves
    • Sound
    • Light & Color
    • Mirrors and Lenses
  • Nuclear Physics
    • Radioactive Decay
    • Half-Life
  • Thermodynamics
    • Temperature, Pressure, and Volume
  • Fluids
    • Archimedes’ Principle
    • Density
